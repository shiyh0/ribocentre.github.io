## Description

***

<font size=4>Group II introns are one of the largest ribozymes in nature, with sequences ranging from 400 to 1000 nt in length. It is also the evolutionary ancestor of the spliceosome. Group II introns can catalyze RNA self-splicing through an autocatalytic two-step reaction and mediate reverse-splicing. It plays an important role in the metabolism of organisms and is now being developed as a tool in biotechnology and gene therapy.</font><br>
